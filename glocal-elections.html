Structuring a researcher's raw findings into a public site

A volunteer researcher had two decades of federal spending data and a genuine policy question, but only a rough written brief describing what he wanted. My job was to turn that brief into an actual information architecture, navigation, and site structure someone could follow start to finish.

Information Architecture Responsive Layout Data Storytelling
RoleUX Lead
FocusIA, Layout & Dashboard Structure
SubjectFederal Elections & Government Spending
Problem

A research brief, not a website

The researcher’s own project documentation was explicit about the gap: the brief covered his data sourcing, his analysis method, and a rough wishlist of sections — homepage, documentation, analysis, visualizations, sources, acknowledgements — but very little about how those pieces should actually connect, or what order a visitor should encounter them in.

“For now, just give me the general structure.”— project documentation, written to the volunteer designer

The underlying research was genuinely strong: a study of the correlation between federal election outcomes and shifts in government spending priorities, built to make an abstract civic question — does who we vote for actually change where public money goes? — legible to ordinary Canadians, not just policy analysts.

Approach

Design the path before the pages

Working from the brief, I defined a five-section structure that mirrors how someone actually wants to consume a research project: arrive, get oriented, read the findings, explore the data, then verify the sources.

Homepage as a thesis, not a table of contents

A minimal entry point stating the project’s central question in one line, with a single clear path into the deeper content — instead of listing every section up front.

Documentation as the trust layer

Methodology, definitions, and an honest limitations section live together, so a skeptical reader can verify the work before trusting the narrative built on top of it.

Analysis & Report as the actual story

Structured like a long-form piece, with supporting charts placed directly beside the claims they support, rather than separated into a disconnected appendix.

Visualizations as their own destination

For readers who want to explore the data directly rather than follow the narrative, each chart gets room to breathe — one or two per screen, not a cramped dashboard grid.

Outcome

A structure the researcher could actually build on

The resulting site ships all five sections as a coherent, navigable whole rather than a pile of loosely related pages — giving the underlying research a shape that matches how people actually read it.
