Rethinking how people find their way around GLOCAL

The current site lists fourteen top-level menu items and scatters its three core programs across the navigation. This prototype shows the problem, proposes a cleaner structure, and lets reviewers test it through a short survey.

Three programs live under one menu. Everything a volunteer needs to start sits under "Get Involved." Nothing hides only in the footer.
1

Too many top-level choices

Fourteen items force visitors to scan the whole bar before acting. Six labelled groups let them predict where things live.

2

Programs are scattered

Online Volunteering, Microgrants, the Essay Contest, and YouCount each sit on their own. Grouping them under "Programs" makes the offer legible at a glance.

3

"Get Involved" competes with the programs

Right now it overlaps the program links. It becomes the single, obvious starting point for a new volunteer.

4

Menu and footer disagree

Reports and Explore appear only in the footer. The proposal pulls reporting into Resources so navigation is consistent everywhere.

Pilot survey · stage one

A trimmed first-stage survey that tests where people expect things to live — before they ever see the menu. Click through it as a reviewer would.

Walkthrough scenario for reviewers

Hand this to a stakeholder. It frames the user, the task, the steps, and what success looks like — so everyone reviews the prototype the same way.

U
The user

Maya, 22, first-time visitor

A student who heard GLOCAL gives small grants for community projects. She has never used the site and wants to apply.

G
Her goal

Find and start a $5,000 grant application

She lands on the homepage with one job in mind: figure out where grants live and how to begin.

1
Step one

Scan the menu

On the live site she meets fourteen links and hesitates. In the prototype she sees six, and "Programs" reads as the likely home for grants.

2
Step two

Open Programs → Microgrants

The grouped menu confirms her guess on the first click. The breadcrumb shows Programs › Microgrants.

3
Step three

Start the application

The Microgrants page leads with a clear "Apply" action rather than burying it in body text.

✓
Expected outcome

Maya reaches the application in two clicks

Success = she never uses search, never backtracks, and can describe afterwards why each step made sense. The survey captures whether real users predict the same paths.
